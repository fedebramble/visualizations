The impact of small group online tutoring in 2023

An analysis based on 38,000 hours of online tutoring delivered to 4,621 students by Pearson via Bramble
1 September 2022 – 31 August 2023
Download Report

2023 Executive Summary

  • An unprecedented volume and depth of data points were gathered and cross-referenced, capturing:
    • user sentiment and attitudes;
    • pre- and post-tuition assessments;
    • tuition block scheduling and attendance;
    • subjects, topics and materials covered in-session;
    • student engagement within live sessions and with post-session revision.
  • Significant improvements in student assessments were achieved across all key stages and subjects.
  • A wide range of subjects and topics are being taught and measured via online tuition, going beyond the key subjects of English and Maths.
  • High levels of interaction by tutors and students, during live sessions and post-session revision, enhanced their confidence and learning.
  • Students receiving Pupil Premium achieved assessment improvements on par with, and often exceeding, their respective cohorts.
  • Attitudes towards online tuition by students and tutors are extremely positive and continuing to increase over time.

Positive student & tutor attitudes towards online tuition is increasing

We surveyed 8,200 teachers, tutors, students and parents over the last 4 years - the most comprehensive study of its kind - to understand their experiences of online tutoring, the perceived impact and benefits, and how these sentiments are changing over time.
